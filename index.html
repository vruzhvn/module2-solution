Our Menu

Beshbarmaq

Beshbarmak is a hearty dish made from boiled meat (usually lamb, beef, or horse meat) served on top of large, flat noodles. The meat is typically cooked with onions, and sometimes it’s served with a rich broth called shorpa, which is poured over the noodles.

Quyrdaq

Quyrdaq is a traditional dish made from fried meat and offal, such as liver, heart, and kidneys, usually from lamb or horse. The meat is often cooked with onions and seasoned with salt and pepper.

Bauyrsaq

Bauyrsaq are small, fried dough balls that resemble doughnuts but are typically less sweet. They’re made from a simple dough of flour, yeast, milk, and butter, then deep-fried to a golden brown.
